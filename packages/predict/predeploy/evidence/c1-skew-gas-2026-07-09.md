# Skew-Adjusted Pricing Gas Findings — 2026-07-09

**Item:** C-1 · **Instrument:** `batch-max-book` A/B (localnet harness) · **Date:** 2026-07-09

Status: measured finding for the SVI skew-adjusted digital pricing change
(DBU-545, PR #1110). Re-validates C-1's post-memo capacity model against the
new `compute_nd2` (adds one `normal_pdf` = one `exp` eval, a slope
`div_scaled`/`mul_scaled` chain, and an i64 clamp per pricing eval).

## The change

`pricing::compute_nd2` now returns the skew-adjusted digital
`N(d2) − φ(d2)·w'(k)/(2·√w)` clamped to `[0, 1e9]` instead of bare `N(d2)`.
Every priced boundary tick pays the additional `normal_pdf` (one `exp`) plus
the slope arithmetic; the memo model is unchanged (one eval per distinct
boundary tick per flush, correction walk reads the cache).

## Method

Same instrument as `c1-price-memo-2026-07-01.md`: harness `batch-max-book`
(single market, fills toward the 5,000 `EMaxActiveLeveragedOrders` cap) with
`SIM_GAS_BUDGET=50000000000`, keeper flush measured against the 5,000M-unit
computation wall. Both sides run on the same machine/toolchain on 2026-07-09
(the 07-01 numbers predate toolchain drift and are quoted for continuity
only). Runs are timeout-bounded at 3600s; both sides reached the full
5,000-order book. Correctness gate: zero `pricing:13`
(`ETickNotInPriceMemo`) aborts and a clean bug oracle.

## Results

| | main @ 462713b5 (bare N(d2)) | PR #1110 (skew-adjusted) | delta |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| per-order flush slope | ~479,879 comp/order (+25.3M base) | ~490,629 comp/order (+53.1M base) | +2.2% |
| full-book (5,000) flush | ~2,472M comp (49% of wall) | ~2,553M comp (51% of wall) | +3.3% |
| extrapolated OOG book size | ~10,366 orders | ~10,082 orders | −2.7% |

Both sides: 60 flushes, peak book 5,000, NAV stable, identical abort profile
(`market_manager:5`, 2× `pricing:9`, 7–8× `expiry_cash:0`). For continuity: the
07-01 evidence measured ~530K/order and 47–54% at full book on the pre-drift
toolchain — both of today's sides land inside that historical band.

## Correctness

- Zero `ETickNotInPriceMemo` on the skew side; NAV stable (no >1% drawdown).
- Bug oracle: 8× `expiry_cash:0` (`EInsufficientCash`) — the same expected
  capital-limit precondition class documented in `c1-price-memo-2026-07-01.md`;
  not a pricing abort.
- Expected-guard aborts: `market_manager:5` (`ECadenceWindowExceeded`, keeper
  cadence-roll timing race) and 2× `pricing:9` (`ELivePricingExpired`, mint
  racing market expiry). Neither touches the formula change.

## Implications for C-1

- The mint path (2 nd2 evals) shows no attributable regression on the CI bench
  (computation within the cross-PR noise band).
- Flush capacity: the skew term costs +2.2% per-order slope / +3.3% at full
  book. The single-market conclusion is unchanged — the 5,000
  `EMaxActiveLeveragedOrders` cap binds, not NAV computation (51% of wall at
  full book).
- Compute-only pool-total envelope: the ~60%-of-wall guidance shrinks
  proportionally to ≈ 5,500–6,000 leveraged orders across all live markets on
  the cheap branch (was ~5,600–6,100), conditional on remaining below the
  transaction object-cache footprint. C-1's object-cache ceiling binds first
  when orders introduce enough distinct payout nodes; this measurement only
  refreshes the computation-cost component of that capacity model.
